Duckworth by Kendrick Lamar

Kendrick Lamar's "Duckworth", is the final track on Kendrick Lamar's album DAMN. It tells the story of a cricital encounter between Kendrick Lamar's father aka Ducky, and Anthony Tiffith aka "Top Dawg", the future CEO of Lamar's record label. Years before Lamar's birth, Ducky worked at a KFC and showed kindness to at the time gang member Tiffith, by giving him extra food, which led Tiffith to spare Ducky's life during a robbery. This act of kindness eventually led to Tiffith discovering and signing Kendrick Lamar, changing his life forever. The song protrays how small acts can have big impacts, shaping lives in unexpected ways.

Duckworth's Song Samples

Duckworth's beat, created by the producer 9th Wonder, has 7 samples from other songs. These 7 samples are spread out throughout the song creating many switch ups which Kendrick Lamar utilizes by switching flows and parts of the story along with the beat.

DAMN.'s Awards

Kendrick Lamar's "Duckworth" was one of many songs on the DAMN. album, which ending up winning 5 Awards at the 60th GRAMMY Awards.

List of Grammy Awards

  • Best Rap Album ("DAMN.")
  • Best Rap/Sung Collaboration ("LOYALTY")
  • Best Rap Song ("HUMBLE")
  • Best Rap Performance ("HUMBLE")
  • Best Music Video ("HUMBLE")
